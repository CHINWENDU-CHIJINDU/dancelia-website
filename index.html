History

Dancelia Children's Home and Foundation is a non-profit organization founded by the children of His Royal Highness Late Eze D.U. Ihewulezi and Late Ugoeze Cecilia Ihewulezi, rulers of Okwuato Autonomous Community in Aboh Mbaise, Imo State, Nigeria.

Their legacy of taking in homeless children and offering unconditional care laid the foundation for what would become a sanctuary for children in desperate need. The name "Dancelia" is a fusion of Daniel and Cecilia, a tribute to their life of service. Officially recognized by the Federal Republic of Nigeria, the foundation is now managed by the Sisters of the Daughters of the Charity of the Most Precious Blood.

Mission

  • Care for children from birth to 17 years without guardians.
  • Empower young women to avoid unplanned pregnancies.
  • Support pregnant women who choose life over abortion.
  • Collaborate with government bodies on fostering and adoption.
  • Educate and nurture every child under our care.
  • Uphold the sacredness of human life and inspire hope.

Core Values

  • Protection of Life: We honor every life entrusted to us.
  • Compassion: We act with love, dignity, and respect.
  • Foster Care: We provide safe, nurturing environments for children.
  • Education: We invest in the future through holistic development.

Admissions & Care Categories

  • Abandoned or homeless children (0 to 17 years).
  • Children temporarily placed by family members under care agreements.
  • Unmarried expectant mothers seeking shelter until childbirth with planning agreements and guardian consent for minors.
